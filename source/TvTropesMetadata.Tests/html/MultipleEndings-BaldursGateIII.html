With the countless choices the player can make, there are essentially thousands of endings, with many being a slight permutation of one another.

Due to the nature of the trope, all spoilers below are unmarked.
    open/close all folders 

    General 
  • Hero of the Forgotten Realms: Prince Orpheus, the Emperor or Karlach succeeds in bringing the Netherbrain to heel, and destroys it along with all the mind flayer tadpoles that had been implanted onto its victims. Baldur's Gate is spared from destruction and the Cult of the Absolute's armies swiftly crumble.
  • Absolute Power Corrupts: Before Prince Orpheus, the Emperor or Karlach can subjugate the Netherbrain, the player character betrays them and takes control of the Netherbrain, essentially taking over the Cult's plans for themselves in a bid for world domination. Patch #7 would later expand on this and the Sins of the Father ending by including new cinematics and variations exclusive to whichever Origin character you picked as your Player Character: Lae'zel uses her newfound army to wage war against Vlaakith, Gale ascends to godhood and intends to use the Cult of the Absolute as his army to subjugate or destroy his rival deities, Astarion props himself up as a "Sun God" who saved Baldur's Gate but is instead a Shadow Dictator expanding his vampiric empire, Wyll takes control of Baldur's Gate and usher a new era of peace by any means necessary and turns his father and several others into mind flayers with the implication he'll use them in his new army, Karlach opens up portals to Avernus and unleashes a horde of devils upon Baldur's Gate as retribution for their blind faith of Gortash and turning her into an outcast, and Shadowheart implies she'll wage war against both Sel没ne and Shar with the Cult of the Absolute and ascend to greater heights before condemning her former Sharran cloister to death. Additionally, if you've consumed too many tadpoles, you'll be forced to pass a Constitution ability check to resist the Netherbrain's attempts to turn you into a mind flayer in a last-ditch attempt to save itself; fail, and the Netherbrain commences its plan to enact the Grand Design.

Character-Specific Endings

    Dark Urge 
  • Sins of the Father: It plays out similarly to the "Absolute Power Corrupts" Ending, but whereas other Origin characters take control of the Netherbrain for their own purposes, the Dark Urge corrupts the Netherbrain and everyone connected to it with their murderous impulses, exactly as Bhaal intended for them to do from the very beginning. You then either command your companions to take a swan dive off the Netherbrain, or you leave them alive so that they can witness the Dark Urge begin an indiscriminate massacre of everyone inside Baldur's Gate. Bhaal then reveals that this carnage is just the beginning of the all-encompassing bloodbath that is to follow, and shows the Dark Urge a vision of them walking alone in a world reduced to blood, guts, and darkness.
  • If you reject Bhaal completely, he will rip out their divine essence, killing them instantly. However, Withers will be impressed and revive you as a normal person without the Urge, claiming you have grown enough as a person to be MORE than a simple bhaalspawn.
  • If you reject Bhaal but control the Netherbrain, the Dark Urge decides to take power for themselves rather than daddy.
  • Accepting the Slayer form but destroying the Netherbrain gives you the option of suicide to keep your friends safe from yourself and Bhaal. Withers appreciates this and keeps your soul from dissolving away, saying your story isn't over yet.
  • Finally, losing to Orin in a duel causes Bhaal to get annoyed and take away your Slayer form, but also begin eroding your mind as above.
  • In all endings where you remain Bhaal's thrall, but destroy the Netherbrain and refuse to commit suicide or be imprisoned, Bhaal's influence will cause the Dark Urge to become a feral shell of their former self, who arrives at Withers' victory celebration six months later to kill everyone.

    Astarion 
The resolution of Astarion's personal quest ends with Cazador's death, one way or another, but how he chooses to go about it can greatly affect his Character Development, for better or for worse.
  • If Astarion hijacks Cazador's ritual and becomes the Vampire Ascendant (which means sacrificing thousands of other vampire spawn in the process), he'll quickly let his newfound power go to his head and become no different than his former master. It's worse if he's romanced, as any romantic feelings he may have had for the Player Character quickly turn into possessive ownership in the most literal sense, and he will even break things off if they become a mind-flayer. This can be downplayed if he's the Player Character, in which case he can abandon his plans to take over Baldur's Gate or even join Karlach in Avernus to protect her — something that a spawn Astarion can't do.
  • If he doesn't take over the ritual, he will instead kill Cazador in a fit of rage. However, as he chooses to remain a vampire spawn, he loses his ability to walk in daylight after the tadpole is destroyed, robbing him of his greatest joy and hampering his ability to be with his friends and/or loved one. He even tearfully notes "it was nice while it lasted" before diving for cover.

    Gale 
  • Gale's questline can end in either Aesop Amnesia where he's learned absolutely nothing and wants to use the Crown of Karsus to become a god — which will end in his death if he immediately tries to usurp Mystra's domain (though Patch 5 does add a new ending where he succeeds by choosing the domain of ambition instead) — or he'll give it to Mystra in the hopes it can be used to cure or, at the very least alleviate, his "affliction".
  • He can also sacrifice his life by blowing himself up to take out the Netherbrain, which leads everyone, even the githyanki, to declare him a hero.

    Karlach 
No matter what you do, Karlach's questline will end in one of three ways when her infernal engine finally sputters out, and none of them are a happy ending, or at least not a golden one.
  • She'll either choose to go out peacefully in Faer没n, happy she died out of Zariel's reach or go back to Avernus to keep the infernal engine going. In the latter, the Player Character can choose to go with her, or Wyll will go with her so as to protect her from Zariel's henchmen, or they can both accompany her together.
  • Alternatively, it's possible for Karlach to become a mind-flayer herself and render her infernal engine useless, though she will now have to deal with the Horror Hunger that comes with her new condition.

    Lae'zel 
  • Even after the truth about Vlaakith's reign is revealed, the player can still go out of their way to screw Lae'zel over and have her remain fanatically loyal to Vlaakith, which inevitably ends in her doom via "ascension". Lae'zel will also remain loyal to Vlaakith by default if you don't do her Creche questline.
  • Even if you convince her to turn back on her faith, Lae'zel's view of the githyanki and its leadership are irreparably shattered. In addition to this, her standing with the githyanki changes depending on whether you side with Prince Orpheus or the Emperor toward the end of the game.
    • If you side with Prince Orpheus, Lae'zel's experiences being on the receiving end of the githyanki's xenophobic behavior for associating with the party and seeing first-hand how corrupt the githyanki are (or at least the majority) makes her realize her kind is not perfect or as proud as she thought they were.
    • If you side with the Emperor, Prince Orpheus' supporters decry her for regicide and are out for her blood, with the implication being she'll be hunted by them for the rest of her life.

    Shadowheart 
Regardless of how you pursue Shadowheart's questline, she will not end up in a good place, particularly where the end of her personal quest is concerned.
  • If she commits to the Sharran priesthood and becomes a Dark Justiciar, her final test sees her standing in front of her parents. Shar restores her memories of who they are and tells her that her final test is to kill them. Shadowheart either acquiesces, which breaks her so completely that she begs Shar to erase her memories again, or turns against Shar at the last minute to save her parents, causing her to suffer Shar's curse for the rest of her life and lose the support of the Sharran cult she'd just taken over. Shadowheart's loyalty to Shar will also affect your romance with her by the endgame, as she becomes more distant thanks to dealing with Shar's demands.
  • Alternatively, you can convince Shadowheart to turn away from Shar, converting to her counterpart and rival Sel没ne in the process and becoming an enemy of the Sharran priesthood. Like in the evil path, Shadowheart finds her parents, but both Shar and they confirm that the only way to be free of Shar is to kill them. Either she does and is freed at the cost of her parents' lives, or she saves them but remains bound to Shar's curse, though she considers this a minor inconvenience compared to getting her family back.
  • Like with Wyll, it's also possible for Shadowheart's story to end horribly in the Sel没nite path if you cut a deal with Viconia and sell her out to the cult, which will cause her to lose all her memories, even those of your party and everything you've been through together, and become completely subservient to Viconia and Shar.

    Wyll 
  • It's possible for Wyll's story to end horribly if you kill Mizora during Act 2, in which case Wyll will be dragged off to Avernus with her and suffer a Fate Worse than Death.
  • Even if Mizora doesn't die and the player helps Wyll in convincing her to break the contract, Mizora will find a way to weasel out of it by providing Wyll with a Sadistic Choice in Act 3: either he renews his pact into an eternal one and saves his father, or he breaks it but condemns his father to death.
    • If the contract isn't broken, Wyll ensures his father's safety, but in return, he becomes Mizora's servant in full and "assists" her in fighting the Blood War.
    • Wyll can Take a Third Option by breaking the pact once and for all but still managing to rescue his father, in spite of Mizora's attempts to stymie his efforts.
  • His actions regarding both his patron and his father can also influence how he'll move forward with his life after the Absolute is dealt with. He can continue his adventures as the Blade of Frontiers, or turn away from monster-hunting and take up his father's mantle as the Grand Duke of Baldur's Gate, or rechristen himself as the Blade of Avernus. And provided that he ended his contract with Mizora, it's only the very latter scenario that gives him the option of getting some long-awaited revenge on her.

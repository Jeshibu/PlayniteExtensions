Multiple Endings / Survival Horror Games
1 Following

  • Create Subpage

Survival Horror games tend to feature at least two different possible endings depending on the players' choice of decisions and actions throughout the gameplay. Bad endings usually lead to the death of Player Character (and sometimes True Companions). Good endings usually lead to Player Character (and their friends) surviving the ordeals.

As with all Ending Tropes, beware of spoilers.

  • Aka Manto has a good and bad ending. In the bad ending, you escape but the killer is safe and people in the future are not. In the good ending, you defeat the killer for good.
  • Amnesia: The Dark Descent has four total endings; one obtained if Daniel doesn't escape after being captured just prior to the endgame and three depending on how the final confrontation is handled. The "bad" ending occurs if either Daniel doesn't escape the cell or he allows Alexander to go through the portal. The Shadow will consume him, tormenting him with the screams of his victims and trapping him in darkness forever. The "good" ending occurs if Daniel stops Alexander's ritual; the Shadow will consume Alexander but spare Daniel, and Daniel leaves Brennenburg feeling redeemed. The "best" ending has Daniel send Agrippa's head through the portal to reunite with Weyer. The Shadow will consume Daniel, but Agrippa's voice will call out to him and promise that everything will be alright while lights appear in the distance.
  • Ao Oni: Version 5.2 had two endings:
    • The Bad Ending: Hiroshi takes the way out via rope ladder immediately and flees the mansion without his friends. The closing epilogue reveals that he never saw any of them again, confirming their deaths.
    • The Good Ending: Hiroshi goes back to collect his friends, but finds the Oni has destroyed the rope ladder. Eventually, they find an alternate route outside and escape together. The ending has Takuro and Hiroshi reflecting on events, hoping it was all a bad dream, while Takeshi has a nightmare about the Oni killing him. The final segment of the ending has a new group enter the house, the camera cutting to outside when the Oni appears. Hiroshi's epilogue states that you should never be as foolish as they were; entering an abandoned mansion may invoke the wrath of the previous owner... especially when no man lives there any more.
  • Bevel's Painting has nine endings, and Word of God says none of them are true. There is also a tenth ending, which is achieved in the Secret Level.
    • Long Dream: The easiest ending to get. Achieved by exiting through the shed without the lit candle in possession. Bevel comes out of her painting absolutely exhausted.
    • Meaningless Illusion: The second easiest ending to get. Achieved by exiting through the dirty door (or the clean door if you ignored other Bevel). Bevel enters and exits a locker. She is back in the real world, completely exhausted.
    • A Rousing Success: This and the above endings are the only ones that can be achieved in more than one branch. Achieved by exiting the clean door without ever seeing the other Bevel. Bevel gets chased by various enemies and enters a white door, leading to a blank space. After some time, the Reaper and the other Bevel appear and corner her, but then they lead her to a banquet and turn into the bunny and fairy from the beginning.
    • And I'm Gone: A Downer Ending. Achieved by dropping the mirror shard in the toy box, getting the black paint, exiting through the shed, and burning the painting. Bevel burns away her painting, along with herself and the world she painted. The ending screen shows that the painting Bevel entered has disappeared from the real world.
    • Dead Inside: Another Downer Ending. Achieved by dropping the mirror shard in the toy box, using the candle to get to the Black World, exiting through the fireplace, and stabbing the heart with scissors. Bevel turns into her counterpart and walks away, and the camera pans to the bleeding heart.
    • Revenge: A Downer Ending that is in the same branch as the good endings. Achieved by going through the four doors, ignoring the other Bevel, and leaving through the dirty door. Bevel gets a canvas scraper, returns to the real world, and kills the other Bevel and her friends.
    • First and Last Friend: A good ending. Achieved by going through the four doors and saying "right" when the other Bevel asks if they are friends. The Bevels make a toast to a new painting. Whether they are in the real world or the painting world is left ambiguous.
    • The Real Me: One of the best endings, and one of the two that actually shows the credits. Achieved by going through the four doors, saying "wrong" when the other Bevel asks if they are friends, and leaving through the clean door. White-haired Bevel tells black-haired Bevel that they are the one and the same, and they merge. Bevel escapes through the canvas and finds herself in the real world, surrounded by her friends.
    • Bevel's Painting: One of the best endings, and the other one that actually shows the credits. After the credits, you are given the code to begin the Secret Level. Achieved by going through the four doors and giving the other Bevel your brush. Black-haired Bevel returns to the real world and paints a picture of white-haired Bevel.
    • Meeting: The ending of the Secret Level. Clearly a direct continuation of "Bevel's Painting". White-haired Bevel runs out of the painting and into black-haired Bevel's bedroom, while black-haired Bevel is watching.
  • Castle Red has six endings. The endings chiefly depend on how Jonathon (the Player Character) interacts with the other characters. Certain endings (E and F) leave many details of the story unexplained.
    • Ending A: "Salvation": Jonathon destroys the worm nest and escapes the castle with Sister Agnes.
    • Ending B: "Lord of the Castle": Jonathon enters the worm nest, bonding with the worms and becoming the new lord. Sister Agnes is forced into a life of servitude.
    • Ending C: "Old Lord Reigns": Howard uses Jonathon to kill Margaery and then disposes of Jonathon so that nobody else can challenge him for the title of Lord.
    • Ending D: "Revenge of the Maid": Margaery uses Jonathon to kill Howard, and then lets him leave.
    • Ending E: "Lost and Damned": Agnes loses all hope, snaps, and begins killing the other characters, starting with Jonathon.
    • Ending F: "Eternal Love": Jonathon joins Cecilia in conquering the castle, and the two rule it together.
  • Chaos Break has four of these:
    • The Golden Ending, after you to successfully escort Rachel, her colleague Sean, and your surviving DEF squad-mate to safety and kill the Final Boss within 2 hours of gameplay. Congrats, the mutation outbreak has been successfully contained, everyone happily boards an evac chopper returning home.
    • The ambiguous ending, if you save Rachel but failed to save Sean, and spend over 2 hours to finish the game. You board an evac chopper and leave, only for the Sand Worm monster that repeatedly harassed you throughout to suddenly attack and attempt chomping you down. It missed, but then you realize the monsters are still on the loose.
    • To get the Bad Ending, let Rachel die and fail your mission. Everyone reaches the evac chopper, only for the Sand Worm monster to suddenly destroy the vehicle, presumably killing everyone.
    • And the Shoot the Shaggy Dog ending; letting Rachel and Sean die ends your game where you escape the facility unscathed, but turns out to be infected by the parasites. Once you return to headquarters, you're shot by your own teammates instantly.
  • Clock Tower has this in spades. The first game, alone, has nine endings:
    • Ending H: Jennifer escapes alone in a car after seeing one of her friends die. Unfortunately, Bobby Barrows is hiding in the backseat, the screen cutting to black just as his scissor blades appear.
    • Ending G: Jennifer escapes alone in a car after seeing two of her friends die. After the credits roll, an epilogue reveals that Jennifer was found dead three days later.
    • Ending F: If Jennifer does not see Lotte die, the game ends when she enters the elevator to the clock tower, as Bobby kills her offscreen, a pool of blood seeping out from below the elevator's doors.
    • Ending E: If Jennifer tries to ride the elevator to the third floor without watching Lotte die first, the elevator suddenly stops, and the screen fades to black as Bobby crashes in through the overhead vent.
    • Ending D: Without realizing Mary's true intentions, Jennifer rushes to her for safety, only for Mary to stab her to death.
    • Ending C: Having realized Mary murdered her father, Jennifer tries to escape from her and run to the clock tower. Mary pursues her up the ladder, but Jennifer manages to kick her off, killing her. Bobby confronts Jennifer in the clock tower, but Jennifer activates the bell on the tower, the noise sending Bobby falling to his death in the gears below.
    • Ending B: This ending is obtained by seeing all of Jennifer's friends die, or by failing to free a crow from a trap. Jennifer immediately makes for the clock tower as Bobby pursues her, activates the bell, and causes Bobby to fall to his demise. Shortly afterwards, Mary attacks Jennifer, but Jennifer manages to shove Mary into the tower's generator, electrocuting her.
    • Ending A: Jennifer finds one of her friends in the clock tower after defeating Bobby, but Mary ambushes them and throws Jennifer's friend to their death. Thankfully for Jennifer, a crow she rescued from a trap earlier appears with its friends to protect her, making Mary fall to her death.
    • Ending S: Jennifer finds one of her friends unconscious in the clock tower before being attacked by Bobby. She uses the bells in the tower to cause Bobby to plummet to his doom. Mary tries to avenge her son's death, but the crow Jennifer rescued returns with friends and attacks Mary, sending her to her doom as Jennifer's friend awakens and they escape together.
  • Each chapter of Corpse Party: Blood Covered has a few "Wrong End"s and a single "True End". Only by reaching the latter can the player continue on to the next chapter. The fact that these are called "True End"s rather than "Good End"s gives you an idea of the general tone of things.
    • The PSP version added a few new endings, including some Extra Ends which end the game off on the weirdest notes, where everything just seems to stop and focus on one character, then throw you back to the title screen to play the chapter again. To add to it, the sequel to the PSP version has the beginning of its story based off of one of the final Bad Ends in Chapter 5.
  • The Count Lucanor has multiple endings depending on whether you sit on the throne or not and choose to go through with certain decisions.
    • The Kobold's Trick: Hans sits on the throne and becomes the new Count, losing all memories of his old life.
    • Pact with the Kobold: Hans sees through Patronio's deception, but becomes the new Count anyway to lift the curse. The now-human Patronio sends some of the castle's riches to Hans' mother, who never sees her son again.
    • Poor But Happy: Hans learns the truth and rejects the throne, leaving the castle with no riches. However, he's helped people along the way and his mother is happy that he's returned safe.
    • Wealth Without Answers: Hans rejects the throne without learning the truth and leaves, but he finds a secret entrance to the castle that grants him riches. He takes whatever he can carry and leaves the rest for Giulia. The legend of the castle still lives on, with many people entering it to find its secrets, but none ever return.
    • Prosperous Future: Hans learns the truth and rejects the throne, but finds the secret entrance to the treasure. This ending plays out like Wealth Without Answers, except Hans and his mother use their wealth to buy the services of a witch who can break the castle's curse, freeing the servants. They then buy the castle themselves and live Happily Ever After.
  • CROWDED. FOLLOWED.
    • Ending One: You get this ending by ignoring the injured man with the briefcase and leaving the alley instead. The homeless man says he knows better than get involved in whatever just happened and says he's "outta this bitch". Cue the credits as cheery orchestral music plays.
    • Endings Two and Three: After finally escaping the entity and boarding the ferry, the homeless man wakes up the following morning safely out at sea, wondering what to do with the briefcase. Here, he has two final choices:
      • Ending Two: The homeless man decides to pass the briefcase to someone else, saying he never wanted it and it's now "someone else's turn". Spotting a little boy with his mother, he convinces the boy to take it, calling it a "mystery puzzle box" and daring him to "solve" it. As he instructs the boy to "share the mystery" with others, the haunting music associated with the entity subtly begins to play, showing the child is now condemned to the same fate, along with anyone else unlucky enough to come into possession of the briefcase.
      • Ending Three: Instead, the homeless man can toss the briefcase into the ocean, vowing to rid himself and others of its curse. He throws it into the water and watches it sink as the bright morning sun shines on him, ready to embrace his fresh start. Seven years later, a fisherman and his son are working at sea, hauling in their catch. Amidst the fish, the father notices a piece of trash tangled in their net. It's the briefcase. As the father tells his son to push it aside, the ominous music begins again; the son picks up the bag, unknowingly inheriting the curse.
  • Dead Rising has seven endings:
    • Ending F (Fail to disarm the bombs in Case 7-2): the mall is destroyed by bombs, which also releases The Virus into the atmosphere, causing zombie outbreaks all across the country.
    • Ending E (Don't solve all the cases, do not be at the helipad on the last day): Frank fails to make it to the helipad for pick-up and is left behind. Otis, Jessie, and any other survivors in the Security Room manage to catch the helicopter before it leaves and escape from the mall.
    • Ending D (Get caught by the Special Forces and remain in their custody): Frank is taken prisoner as the Special Forces clean up the mall and the city. The truth about the outbreak is lost forever.
    • Ending C (Solve all the cases, but don't talk to Isabela at 10 AM on the last day): The helicopter pilot looks for Frank from another building, but can't find him and believes him to be dead. Before he can return to his chopper, however, he is jumped by a zombie. Willamette is placed under quarantine, and the truth of the outbreak is lost.
    • Ending B (Don't solve the cases but be at the helipad on the last day): Frank and the other survivors catch the helicopter and escape from the mall. The cause of the outbreak remains a mystery and cities across America began having similar outbreaks days after Willamette, which is only briefly remembered as the first city to fall.
    • Ending A (Solve all the cases, talk to Isabela, and be at the helipad on the last day): Frank prepares to catch a ride out of the mall, but a zombie managed to get onto the helicopter and attacks the pilot as he makes his approach. The helicopter crashes, leaving Frank stranded and surrounded by zombies...
    • True Ending (Complete Overtime Mode): Frank has been infected, but Isabela helps him synthesize a zombification suppressant. Carlito, Isabela's brother, had already put into motion a plan to spread the zombie outbreaks far and wide. They make their escape, but are pursued by the special forces leader, who was complicit in the destruction of Isabela and Carlito's hometown and seeks to keep the truth from spreading. Frank and Isabela manage to escape, and Frank exposes the US government's development of the zombie parasites. Although the government accepts partial responsibility, they blame the Willamette outbreak on terrorists and the incident soon fades from the public's mind. All the while, the possibility of the infection spreading lingers...
  • Dead Rising 2 has seven endings:
    • Ending F (Katey dies, return to safe house at the end of Day 3): Zombies break into the safe room. Chuck, however, suffers a Heroic BSoD and offers no resistance when the zombies swarm him. No one survives the Fortune City outbreak, now known as the "Greene Incident".
    • Alternate Ending F (Run out of time in Overtime mode): Katey and Stacey die when TK lowers them into a swarm of hungry zombies.
    • Ending D (Do not return to the safe room at the end of Day 3): Chuck is apprehended by the military. As he is being driven through Fortune City, however, the truck comes to a sudden halt as a thick green fog covers the area. No one survives the outbreak following Fortune City's firebombing.
    • Ending C (Fail a Case File before 6-1, Katey survives; or be in the safe room but out of the security office at 10am on Day 3): Chuck, Stacey, and Katey get ready to leave as the military arrives, but hear gunshots and screams. Chuck goes to investigate, but is shot and killed. No one survives the outbreak when the city is firebombed.
    • Ending B (Fail a Case File after 6-1, or run out of time during "The Facts"): Bombs are shown being dropped on Fortune City. No one survives.
    • Ending A (Complete all case files, save Katey, do not give TK Zombrex): After dispatching the final boss, Chuck holds off the firebombing of Fortune City so survivors can be airlifted. While Stacey and Katey board a chopper, Chuck goes to retrieve Katey's backpack, only to be ambushed by a zombified TK and getting himself trapped in an elevator full of other zombies. In the epilogue, Chuck is cleared of the charges against him for instigating the outbreak, thanks to Stacey's testimony, but it is unknown if he survived. The incident is known as "Fortune's End". This ending is canon and leads into Case West and Dead Rising 3.
    • Ending S (Complete all case files, save Katey, give TK Zombrex): The ending starts off like Ending A, but differs when Chuck goes to retrieve Stacey and Katey but finds them missing. This leads into Overtime mode, where Chuck is forced to retrieve items for TK in exchange for Stacey and Katey's safety. TK dies in the ensuing conflict between him and Chuck, and Chuck walks away from Fortune City with Stacey and Katey. Enjoy your Jump Scare.
  • Dead Rising 2: Off The Record has all the endings from the vanilla version of the game except for Ending F (since Frank doesn't need to give Zombrex to anyone, and failure to give himself Zombrex simply kills him) and Ending A. Some of the endings are also changed due to the game's focus on Frank instead of Chuck.
  • Dead Rising 3 continues the trend of multiple endings.
    • Ending F (Do not finish the chapters within the time limit): Los Perdidos is firebombed, and all organic life in the city is destroyed. In the aftermath, General Hemlock completes a powerful bio-weapon and places America under martial law.
    • Ending D (Complete Chapters 1-6, go to the plane instead of the karaoke bar): Nick decides it's every man for himself and tries to escape Los Perdidos on his own. Annie, Red, Rhonda, and Gary stop him. Nick tries in vain to convince them he was just joking. Nick is left to die, and General Hemlock proceeds to cause more zombie outbreaks across the country.
    • Ending C (Complete all Chapters, kill Gary): Nick, Annie, Dick, and Isabela make for their escape, but the plane stalls. Zombies swarm the plane, and they all die. General Hemlock is arrested, but no cure for zombification is found.
    • Ending S (Complete all Chapters, reunite Gary and Rhonda): After Red's death, Annie's father arrives: none other than Chuck Greene, meaning Annie is actually Katey Ann Greene. Before they can leave the city, though, they hear over the plane's radio of Hemlock's plan to create a bio-weapon with zombies to create zombie outbreaks, then kill all the zombies while leaving the city intact, like a neutron bomb. Nick thwarts Hemlock's plan to harvest king zombies and kills him in an ensuing fight, then evacuates Los Perdidos and uses his blood to create the cure for zombification. In The Stinger, it is revealed that Isabela convinced Phenotrans CEO Marian Mallon to start the Los Perdidos outbreak, so Nick would discover he was the cure. With that, the Keyes family name is cleared of Carlito's actions in Willamette. The scene ends with Isabele destroying the evidence of her involvement.
  • While the base game of Dead Rising 4 has only one ending, the epilogue DLC "Frank Rising" has three endings:
    • Good ending (collect all princess wasps before escaping): Frank escapes from Willamette before the town is firebombed with Dr. Blackburne, Hammond, and their allies. Afterwards, Frank and Vick expose the US government's involvement with the second Willamette outbreak. Frank also publishes a book about his experiences as a zombie, which becomes a best seller and helps him become a star once again.
    • Bad ending (do not collect all princess wasps before escaping): Frank escapes from Willamette before the town is firebombed with Dr. Blackburne. Hammond and her friends, sadly, perish. Afterwards, Frank retires and becomes a recluse, never revealing to anyone his experiences as a zombie and haunted for the rest of his life by nightmares.
    • Worst ending (run out of time): Willamette is firebombed. Blame for the outbreak is directed away from the government and towards terrorists. There are no survivors.
  • Death Park: The game's ending can be influenced by your actions and choices.
  • The first Dino Crisis has three different endings. In the last branching path in the game, the heroine Regina must choose between letting Dr. Kirk escape or accompanying a severely wounded Gail (her commanding officer) to capture him. In the former choice, the three heroes escape together, but they fail their mission for letting Dr. Kirk escape. In the second choice, Dr. Kirk is captured, but Gail succumbs to his wounds, who reveals before dying that the true objective of their mission was not the capture of Dr. Kirk, but the data disc he was carrying, which contains the research data of the Third Energy project. However, there's also a third alternative: Regina can leave Gail under Rick's care, only to go after Dr. Kirk by herself, allowing all four characters to escape.
  • The Echo Night series has a set of endings in each of the three games in the series, each with slightly different criteria to achieve the different endings.
    • Echo Night 1's endings are based on whether you escape the Orpheus at the end of the game and whether you help all of the ghosts pass on and accept the Soul Stone from the nameless medium.
      • Fail to escape: Richard goes missing, and the truth of what happened remains hidden from the world.
      • Escape, but don't talk to the medium: Richard escapes and goes to leave the mansion, but the Soul Stone is found in the backseat of his car...
      • Escape and talk to the medium, accept the Soul Stone: Richard escapes and prepares to leave the mansion, stabbing a police officer in the back on his way out, continuing the Stone's bloody history...
      • Escape and refuse the Soul Stone: The medium destroys the stone, wishing you a bright future, and Richard escapes, content knowing that the souls trapped in the limbo the Orpheus was stuck in are free and the Soul Stone can no longer hurt anybody.
    • Echo Night: Lord Of Nightmares endings are based on the percentage of Astral Pieces collected from helping ghosts pass on, but it's possible to get an alternate version of the standard ending if the player is too slow in the final segment.
      • Standard Ending 1: Jessica shoots Albert after he shoots Richard, sick of him killing for her sake. Richard and Christina get married and have twins three years later, but the Soul Stone had gone missing since Albert's death...
      • Standard Ending 2: Albert makes it to the clock tower first, wounding Richard with a gunshot when he arrives and sees Jessica and Christina collapsed on the ground. Albert tosses aside his weapon and attempts to finish Richard off with the Soul Stone, but gets shot from behind with his own gun, seemingly by one of the women. Richard then blacks out before waking up in the hospital, brought there by one of the sisters who claims to have lost her memory of what happened. The exact fate of the twins are unknown; Albert believes Jessica shot him, but Richard claims his wife is Christina, and only one woman is seen during the ending...
      • Good Ending (All Astral Pieces): Albert is shot by his accomplice after he shoots Richard, and the accomplice offers Richard the Soul Stone. After refusing itnote Accepting just gives a Non-Standard Game Over where she stabs Richard with the Stone, the medium appears and after a conversation between the medium and the accomplice, the accomplice breaks the Stone. Jessica recovers from her illness and Richard and Claudia get married.
      • Evil Ending (No Astral Pieces): Albert tries to shoot Richard, but Christina takes the bullet and dies, which causes Albert to kill himself since he needed to sacrifice Christina to the Soul Stone to make Jessica immortal. Richard takes the Soul Stone and kills Jessica with it to save Christina.
    • Echo Night Beyond has the player make two choices: A Last-Second Ending Choice where Richard accepts an offer to obtain the Soul Stone from Claudia's ghost and whether he takes some keepsakes belonging to Claudia and the real Richard from a room unlocked by helping all of the ghosts pass on.
      • Accept Offer, Take Keepsakes: Claudia sees this as the android trying to have everything and making the same mistakes the real Richard had that led to the colony's ruin. She gives the android a "The Reason You Suck" Speech before passing on, with the android walks past Claudia's body and holds the Stone up as he looks towards Earth.
      • Accept Offer, Ignore Keepsakes: Claudia declares the android the real Richard and passes on. The scene continues similarly to the previous ending until the android then stabs himself with the Stone to try to bring Claudia back, but it fails.
      • Refuse Offer, Ignore Keepsakes: Claudia simply thanks the android before passing on, and the android carries Claudia's body away as the camera pans to the Earth.
      • Refuse Offer, Take Keepsakes: Claudia accepts that her husband is gone and is never coming back, but passes on happily with the memories of the Richard she once knew and loved. The android takes her body, places her ring on her hand, and performs a Burial in Space.
  • Eternal Darkness: Sanity's Requiem has the player controlling several characters throughout history, all bound by the Tome of Eternal Darkness, and all trying to stop the resurrection of an evil, ancient god. There are actually three main gods in the game, each one weak to one of the others (along with a fourth god seen to be more powerful, but magically bound by the others). The evil one is determined in the first chapter when the player is in control of Pious Augustus, a Roman centurion in the 1st century. When he is faced with three different idols, he must choose one, determining the antagonist (as well as determining which one the player has to awaken to defeat it). With each successive playthrough, Pious' choices get whittled down until he's chosen them all. When the player has beaten the game three times (and has therefore beaten each of the gods), the True End is shown, revealing that each playthrough took place in an alternate timeline, and now all three of the gods have been defeated. It's also revealed that the fourth god manipulated the humans into destroying each of its lesser guardians, breaking their hold on him and leaving him the only one in power. This is a case where the True End is combined with the Bad End, as the fourth god is said to now be waiting and plotting.
  • Each of the Fatal Frame games have multiple endings. Usually, the more tragic ones are considered canon.
    • The first game had three endings: one gotten on Easy/Normal, where only Miku escapes, Mafuyu choosing to stay behind, one on Nightmare: both Miku and Mafuyu escape, and one on the Xbox where Kirie is reunited with her lover. Only the first one is canon.
    • Fatal Frame II: Crimson Butterfly had four endings: one where Mio runs away, one gotten on the easier difficulties where Mio sacrifices Mayu to the Hellish Abyss, one for the harder difficulties (Mio and Mayu both escape, but Mio is now blind), and another Xbox exclusive ending where Yae and Sae reunite and Mio and Mayu escape. The second ending is canon.
    • The Wii remake of Crimson Butterfly, in addition to keeping the four mentioned above, adds two new ones: One where Mio refuses to sacrifice Mayu, so Mayu goes Ax-Crazy and kills Mio herself, and another where Mio is too late to stop the Repentance from emerging from the Hellish Abyss, and instead decides to spend her final moments with Mayu (though it's implied they are Together in Death).
    • Fatal Frame III only has two endings. In the first one, only Rei and Miku are implied to have survived. The second ending requires a sidequest to be completed but has everyone surviving. Word of God says the second ending is the canon one.
    • Fatal Frame: Mask of the Lunar Eclipse has two endings: one where Misaki's fate is not revealed and one gotten by beating the Hard difficulty where it's clear she survived.
    • Fatal Frame: Maiden of Black Water does things a little differently, as there are individual endings for each of the three playable characters. There are four possible endings for Ren, two for Miu, and two for Yuuri.
  • FAITH: The Unholy Trinity:
    • Chapter I has five endings depending on what the player shoots with the gun. If you shoot Amy, John is arrested for murder with the reveal that Amy was an escapee from a mental institution and that John had been hallucinating that she was a demon. Maybe. If you shoot a deer with the woods, a group of deer will kill John in return. If you shoot the shadow in the woods, Michael will be waiting to ambush John in the backseat of his car. If you shoot the dead fox in the pentagram, John is jumped by a cult. The best ending is gotten by shooting Michael, who attempts to ambush John as he returns to his car.
    • Chapter II has three endings this time. If the player keeps Father Garcia alive during the boss battle with Sister Bell, John wakes up from a nightmare and monologues in his car about his failure to exorcise Amy and how he needs to save her brothers, who are going to be targeted. Fail to keep Father Garcia alive (or commit at least one of the Unspeakable Evils) and John's monologue becomes less hopeful, and a red figure is seen tailing him. The third ending involves going out of your way to do three terrible things... and this ending involves John being initiated into the Eternal Order of the Second Death.
    • Chapter III has another three endings (with two variants for the first two). The first one is accomplished by completing the game normally - John and Garcia prevent the Profane Sabbath, but Gary escapes to try again and they therefore set off to stop him for good (whether John wants it or not). The second one is the Golden Ending for killing all of the secret bosses, in which Gary is Dragged Off to Hell, Amy is finally freed to enter the afterlife, and John either settles down with Lisa or goes forth with Garcia. The third is a horrifically brutal Downer Ending for completely giving up in which John passes the Despair Event Horizon and lets himself be claimed by the UNSPEAKABLE.
  • Fear & Hunger contains a multitude of possible endings, with some of them only obtainable on Masochist difficulty, and some unobtainable on that same difficulty thanks to the lack of party members.
    • Ending A: The God of Fear And Hunger. First, you need to end up bringing The Girl all the way down to the end of the Gauntlet at the very bottom of the dungeon. Once there, she leaves your group and ends up undergoing a Painful Transformation, snapping and attacking you as she grows into ever more nightmarish forms, until she finishes her transformation into the God of Fear and Hunger. Successfully "winning" against her onslaught will have you collapse of exhaustion and fall to the very bottom of the dungeon, and the new God speaks to you thanking you for her ascent as she puts you out of your misery and begins a new age of cruelty to spur mankind out of Medieval Stasis.
    • Ending B: The Old One. Upon reaching the same area as Ending A without The Girl, the traces of Gro-goroth emerge from the darkness and attack you.       • Ending A: Achieved in Timeline A by correctly performing the ritual and defeating the Scissor Woman. Your friend is saved, and the Scissor Woman is banished.
      • Ending B: Achieved in Timeline A by incorrectly performing the ritual, or burning down the school in a previous mystery, and defeating the Scissor Woman. While the Scissor Woman is banished, but your friend is nowhere to be seen, and you're compelled to take the cursed scissors for yourself...
      • Ending C: A Non-Standard Game Over from losing to the Scissor Woman, who kills you while cutting a Glasgow Grin into your face.
      • Ending D: The only ending available in Timeline B. Just as you confront the Scissor Woman, you awaken in an ambulance, having been found passed out in the classroom. The last thing you see before falling asleep from the medication is old scars on the paramedic's cheeks...
    • Alarming Account of Abnormal Arms:
      • Ending A: The default ending for Timeline A. The root cause of the strange goings-on in the apartment building is the skeevy landlord, who is found dead in the building's vents.
      • Ending B: The default ending for Timeline B. The root cause of the strange goings-on in the apartment building is a monster that has taken over the landlord's body, filling it with centipedes. Naturally, you have to fight it.
    • Bizarre Bruit of the Blood-curdling Botanist:
      • Ending A: Achieved by completing the sidequest and having the Fire Axe from a previous mystery, then obtaining a DIY flamethrower during the ensuing battle. After defeating the botanist's fungus-possessed body, you burn his corpse and van to keep the secrets of the fungus from ever seeing the light of day.
      • Ending B: Achieved by killing the boss normally. You realize the garden that had been attacking you was actually trying to keep the botanist's fungus-possessed body from escaping.
      • Ending C: Achieved by helping the botanist escape. You pass out and awaken in a hospital, but no one has seen the botanist...
      • Ending D: Achieved by letting the botanist die without fighting the boss. As the garden attacks you, you are forced to leave the botanist behind...
    • Chilling Chronicle of a Crimson Cape:
      • Ending A: Achieved by defeating Aka Manto in his true form. While it is too late to save your friend, at the very least you can rest easy knowing Aka Manto will never hurt anyone again.
      • Ending B: Achieved by defeating Aka Manto without revealing his true form. Aka Manto is defeated, but he may return...
      • Ending C: A Non-Standard Game Over from losing to Aka Manto. You are banished to the Otherworld.
    • Curious Case of a Contagious Coma:
      • Ending A: Achieved by revealing the boss with Dust of Seeing and defeating it. The cause behind the comas plaguing the town is forced into retreat, and the victims regain consiousness.
      • Ending B: Achieved by not using the Dust of Seeing. All of the coma victims are dead, which means that the comas should stop...
    • Eerie Episode of Evolving Eels:
      • Ending A: Achieved by finding Kana in the doctor's apartment and jabbing their eye out. While Kana loses their left eye, your quick thinking saves their life.
      • Ending B: Achieved by taking Kana to the hospital. When you awaken after taking them to the hospital, however, everyone acts as though Kana never existed, and it seems you were too late to save your friend...
      • Ending C: Achieved by leaving the doctor's apartment without finding Kana. When the police investigate the apartment the next day, Kana is nowhere to be seen, and never heard from again...
    • Far-Out Fable of a Fear Festival:
      • Ending A: Achieved by entering the Overgrown Factory before dawn. Just as the cult summons a monster to sacrifice the festival attendees, the police raid the factory and save everyone.
      • Ending B: Achieved by entering the Overgrown Factory after dawn. You arrive at the factory to see the cult leader being arrested, but you're too late: the ritual was completed, and the monster is free.
      • Ending C: Achieved by attempting to stop the ritual during the events leading to Ending A. You succeed in stopping the summoning ritual yourself, and barely escape with your life.
    • Freaky Feature of Found Footage:
      • Ending A: Achieved by pouring acid into the well. The house collapses, and you awaken to find the found footage tapes in your hand.
      • Ending B: Achieved by trying to peek into the well. A cultist protecting the house knocks you out and puts you on a bus bound for the city.
      • Ending C: Achieved by taking the tapes from the hut and escaping. When you lead a search party back to the hut, the way into the basement where you found the tapes is gone.
    • Horrible History of Household Hell:
      • Ending A: Achieved by sacrificing a companion to Oetaru. The beast returns to sea, and you destroy the speakers that summoned it and the mansion built over it to keep anyone else from summoning Oetaru.
      • Ending B: Achieved by running from Oetaru. The beast continues to terrorize the coast around Shiokawa.
      • Ending C: Achieved by failing to find Oetaru. The mystery of the corpses in the caves under the mansion is left unsolved...
      • Non-Standard Game Over: Achieved by failing to deal with Oetaru for three turns. The intense noise from Oetaru blows out your eardrums and causes you to hemorrhage to death.
    • Macabre Memoir of Morbid Mermaids:
      • Ending A: Achieved by killing the Mad Janitor. While his body is never found by the police, you rest easy knowing he cannot hurt anyone ever again.
      • Ending B: Achieved by forcing the manhole open while fighting the Mad Janitor. An actual mermaid pulls the Janitor in and kills him. In any case, the Janitor's killing spree is over.
      • Ending C: Achieved by toppling an oil lamp while fighting the Mad Janitor. You manage to kill the Mad Janitor in the inferno, but the school is burned to the ground.
      • Ending D: Achieved by distracting the Mad Janitor when playing as a female character with the Insmasu Curse. You manage to escape while the Janitor is distracted, but he is not seen again...
      • Ending E: Available in Timeline B by sacrificing an ally to the Mad Janitor, who is an actual merman. You're left troubled by the Janitor's words, and you've begun to develop a skin condition that looks suspiciously scaly...
    • Rotten Report of a Rancid Ramen:
      • Ending A: Achieved by finding rancid meat and submitting it for lab testing. Whatever is in this ramen is alive! When you go to confront the owner of the ramen stand, however, he and his shack are gone, with only a sentient piece of meat left behind.
      • Ending B: Achieved by not submitting rancid meat for testing. There is some kind of addicting drug in the ramen. A week later, the ramen stand is gone. Unfortunately, there are reports of similar ramen stands in other cities...
    • Sorrowful Saga of the Moonlight Sailors:
      • Ending A: Achieved by completing the sidequest in Timeline A. The dark beings are not seen again, but the beach sits abandoned, and there are reports of mysterious beings coming to port...
      • Ending B: Achieved by not completing the sidequest in Timeline A. The town's sailors leave for the ocean, never to return...
      • Ending C: Only available in Timeline B. You set fire to the black ooze on the beach.
    • Viscious Verses of a Violent Vigil:
      • Ending A: Achieved by copying the old man's tattoo, following the pamphlet's itinerary, and carving the tattoo's design at dawn. You defeat the old man as he tries to cheat death once again, saving everyone at the vigil.
      • Ending B: Achieved under the same conditions as Ending A, but also sacrificing one of the guests. The old man is defeated, but you awaken to find yourself stained in blood.
      • Ending C: Achieved by sacrificing both guests. The old man manages to complete his end of a deal with eldritch beings to prolong his life, and you are partly responsible!
